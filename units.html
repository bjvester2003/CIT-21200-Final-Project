Age of Empires II

Units

Man at Arms

The Man at Arms is an early game form of basic infantry. An upgraded form of the Militia unit, the Man at Arms is an effective fighter against spearmen and archers. However, it deals bonus damage to buildings and eagle warriors.

The Man at Arms can be trained at a barracks for 60 food and 20 gold.

Longswordsman

An upgraded version of the Man at Arms, the longswordsman shares many of the same bonuses as the Man at Arms. A noteable difference is an additional point of melee armor.

The Man at Arms can be trained at a barracks for 60 food and 20 gold.

Spearman

The spearman is the basic anti-cavalry unit. However, this unit is weak to archer and additional infantry.

The spearmen can be trained at a barracks for 35 wood and 25 gold.

Learn More

Eagle Warrior

A unit that is unique to Central American civilizations, such as the Aztecs and Myans, the eagle warrior is exceptonally fast. As the Central American civilizations have no access to cavalry, eagle warriors act as a substitute for scout cavalry.

The Eagle Warrior can be trained at a barracks for 20 food and 50 gold.

Learn More

Archer

The archer is the basic ranged unit. In small groups, archers are only effective against smaller opponents. However, when properly protected and in Sufficient numbers, archers can be a dangerous foe.

Archers can be trained at an archery range for 25 wood and 45 gold.

Learn More

Elite Skirmisher

The elite skirmisher is the premier anti-archer ranged unit. While the elite skirmisher gains bonus damage against archers and spearmen, they are weak against cavalry and infantry

The elite skirmisher can be trained at an archery range for 25 food and 35 wood.

Learn More

Bombard Cannon

The bombard cannon is a thrilling siege weapon. Like all siege weapons, the bombard cannon can be swiftly destroyed by and foot soldiers. Despite this, the bombard cannon can make short work of walls, ships, carts, and other mechanical constructs.

The bombard cannon can be trained at the siege workshop for 225 wood and 225 gold.

Light Cavalry

The light cavalry is a useful scout unit. Their swift speed and greater field of view allows them to rapidly explore new locations. These units are weaker than other cavalry units so it is best to keep a close eye on them.

The light cavalry can be trained at the stables for 80 food.

Learn More

Winged Hussar

The winged hussar is an upgraded version of the scout cavalry line, unique to the Poles and Lituanians. Overall these units are very similar, but the winged hussar does additional damage to monks and gunpowder units.

The light cavalry can be trained at the stables for 80 food.

Learn More

Cavalry Archer

The cavalry archer obviously combines the range of archers with the speed and manuverability of cavalry units. The cavalry archer is weak to spearmen and skirmishers.

The cavalry archer can be trained at the archery range for 40 wood and 60 gold.

Learn More

Camel Rider

The camel rider is the middle eastern replacement for the knight. The camel rider has less damage than the knight, but is allowed more bonuses.

The camel rider can be trained at a stable for 55 food and 60 gold.

Learn More

Villager

Villagers are the foundation of any economy. They are your hunters, gatherers, miners, and lumberjacks. They are overall weak, but a mob of villagers can make for an interesting engagement.

Villagers can be trained at a town center for 50 food.

Learn More

Trade Cart

The trade cart works to move supplies between the player's market and and a market of an ally. The farther the trade route, the more gold that a Trade Cart will deliver.

The trade cart can be trained at a market for 100 wood and 50 gold.

Learn More

War Galley

The war galley is the basic naval unit. In practice, the war galley is weak on an individual level. In a bigger fleet however, and with support ships, war galleys can properly hamper and port.

The war galley can be trained at a dock for 90 wood and 30 gold.

Learn More

Fast Fireship

The fast fireship is an effective anti-ship ship. It deals effective damage to ships of all kind as well as buildings. However, the fast fireship is not as durable as galleys.

The fast fireship can be trained at a dock for 75 wood and 45 gold.
